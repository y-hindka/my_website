Professional Interests

Robotics

I am passionate about Robotics; it is a powerful tool to make human lives easier and more productive. In my undergradute years, I was on the Wisconsin Robotics team, where I contributed to the autonomous navigation system of our Mars rover prototype (pictured left).

Later on in my college career, I joined the Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) Lab. There, I worked with the Microsoft Hololens to create an AR workspace for a Panda robotic arm. Check out my projects page for more on this. Another project at the HCI involved researching optimal positioning for a camera drone providing a viewpoint for a remote robot operator.

Today, I am part of the Biorobotics Team at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU). Here, I am helping the Medsnake team build out a visualization tool suite. Helping to make a physician's job easier and more effective is something that I feel highly rewarded by, and I hope to be able to build a career around this in the future.

Spurred by my interest in Biorobotics, I have recently realized my passion for medicine. I often find myself cogitating a career where I can combine my love of robotics with medicine. To me, writing software that enables me to improve a patient's experience during treatment is beyond rewarding. This is what drives me to pursue medical school today. I have found such joy in my interactions with patients while volunteering and know that I would be most fulfilled with a career dedicated to enhancing the patient experience.

Computer Vision

I have recently started to experiment more with Computer Vision (CV), and in doing so, I have realized it is something that piques my interest. It relies heavily on n-dimensional math, which is something that challenges me. While I currently only play around with OpenCV on my RaspberryPi, I am hoping to do some research with it in the near future.

From assignments in class to personal projects to professional development, I have a strong interest in AI. I enjoy learning about how math can be applied to a problem to create a model that allows the computer to "think" on its own. I find it compelling how accurate these models can be and am looking for more ways to get involved with it professionally. Although I have done AI projects through coursework and on my own, I am looking to get my foot inside the "AI-door" in the workplace to learn the different ways companies are reaping AI's benefits.
