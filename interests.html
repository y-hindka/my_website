Professional Interests

Robotics

I am passionate about Robotics; it is a powerful tool to make human lives easier and more productive. In my undergradute years, I was on the Wisconsin Robotics team, where I contributed to the autonomous navigation system of our Mars rover prototype (pictured left).

Later on in my college career, I joined the Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) Lab. There, I worked with the Microsoft Hololens to create an AR workspace for a Panda robotic arm. Check out my projects page for more on this. Another project at the HCI involved researching optimal positioning for a camera drone providing a viewpoint for a remote robot operator. In the future, I hope to delve into medical robotics and see what contributions I can make there.

I have recently started to experiment more with Computer Vision (CV), and in doing so, I have realized it is something that piques my interest. It relies heavily on n-dimensional math, which is something that challenges me. While I currently only play around with OpenCV on my RaspberryPi, I am hoping to do some research with it in the near future.

AI

From assignments in class to personal projects to professional development, I have a strong interest in AI. I enjoy learning about how math can be applied to a problem to create a model that allows the computer to "think" on its own. I find it compelling how accurate these models can be and am looking for more ways to get involved with it professionally. Although I have done AI projects through coursework and on my own, I am looking to get my foot inside the "AI-door" in the workplace to learn the different ways companies are reaping AI's benefits.
